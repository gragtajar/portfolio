Lens Package Selection

Revamped designs for lens package selection screen for Lenskart consumer mobile app and its counterpart iPad app for store staff.

7 mins read · Updated on 29 Jun 2025

About Project

Customers' Problems

Struggles to understand and compare lens packages due to overwhelming information, unclear visuals and lack of guidance on what’s best for their needs.

.

Store Staff Problems

Faces difficulty in explaining lens options quickly and clearly to customers, especially during rush hours.

Why Re-design?

Major Pain Points/Insights

  • Too Much Text

    Lens points are dense and hard to scan

  • Missing Context

    No frame or price breakdown shown on lens screen

  • No Comparison Help

    Tough to differentiate amongst lenses

  • Premium lenses go unnoticed

    Customers often pick basic lenses just to move ahead

Data measured

  • 86% of users move from lens screen to cart.
  • But ATV remains low (~₹1500).
  • Premium lenses like Photochromatic & Owndays have less than 1% adoption.

Importance of this screen

It's not just a product listing, it connects users with medically necessary, vision-correcting lenses.

It directly influences both health outcomes and business revenue.

.

Omni-channel Importance

Customers

Using on their mobile app

  • Needs to self-navigate the screen without assistance.
  • Poor hierarchy and dense info create friction and abandonment.
  • Visual consistency helps reinforce decision-making.

Store Staff (iPad app)

Using on iPad app when customers visit stores to buy frames with lenses

  • Relies on the same screen to explain options clearly to walk-in customers.
  • Cluttered layout slows down consultations.
  • A unified experience across app & store builds trust and reduces dependency on staff.

.
